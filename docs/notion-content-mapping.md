# Notion Content Mapping

Use Notion as the content source of truth. Figma placement is based on the planned content slot unless the user says otherwise.

## Common Fields

- `Headline`: usually becomes the cover headline.
- `Content`: source for carousel slides and body sections.
- `Caption`: publishing copy for LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram, or other channels. Do not place this in the design unless requested.
- `Concept`: creative guidance for visual direction, template selection, and emphasis.
- `Deliverable`: indicates format, such as carousel, web, cover, or social post.
- `Languages`: indicates whether English, Spanish, and French versions are needed.
- `Date`: use this to infer the target monthly page and day row unless the user gives a different planned slot.

## Carousel Mapping

For carousel posts:

- Cover: use the headline or a concise version of it.
- Slide 2: establish the problem, tension, or hook.
- Middle slides: split the argument into clear sections, stats, or progression steps.
- Last slide: use implications, takeaway, proof points, or a CTA depending on the source content.

Do not copy long Notion paragraphs mechanically into Figma. Distill the narrative so each slide has a clear job.

## Thought Leadership And Web Posts

If the content is a thought leadership piece or web-published post, create the carousel/social version as needed and also include the square `Web` design when requested or implied by the workflow.

If unsure whether `Web` is needed, infer from the deliverable, channel, and content type. Ask only if the implication is unclear and the decision affects scope.

## Language Handling

Notion may only contain English source content even when `ESP` and `FRA` are listed. In that case:

- Translate from the approved English design, not directly from the raw Notion text.
- Preserve meaning and hierarchy.
- Shorten translated copy if needed for layout.
- Keep technical terms consistent, such as `PAPSS`, `RTGS`, `IPS`, `ISO 20022`, `GPH`, `STP`, `VAM`, and `SWIFT`.
